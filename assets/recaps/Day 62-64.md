# McAllen Recap

# Finalist and Sustainability Award winners\!\!\!

# McAllen Recap

- We loaded in at Mcallen a little later than we wanted due to the drive taking a bit longer. But we got in, passed inspection and were ready to go fairly quickly. We didn’t have to do much work, if any, on the robot on load-in night so we were able to leave early, get dinner and get to bed early.

# McAllen Day 1

- Since the event was a small one, we actually got around 3 hours of practice matches which was super beneficial for us. It allowed us to get a good feel of the game before the competition started and fine tune some things. Our biggest issue initially was getting our POSE working correctly, then getting the PID tuned for our shots. PID tuning took a lot longer than we wanted so we weren’t dialed in until Day 2 unfortunately. We managed to finish Day 1 with a 6-2 record and ranked 3 by the end of the night.

- In our scouting meeting, we knew a lot of our focus was going to be on finding a good second pick due to the field being so small. We did some calculations and match predictions and came to the conclusion that we were gonna be ranked 3rd by the end of the event if everything went as expected. Our first pick was between 10385, Cycle-Breakers and 4063, TriKzR4Kidz. Both were pretty good shooters but both had also had some rough shooting on Day 1\. Their EPA was around the same with 4063 having a slight advantage. We knew both had some mechanical issues throughout the day so we wanted to watch some matches on Saturday and see how they were fairing. We also had a match with 10385 at the end of the qualification schedule that we were going to use to gauge how we played together. 

- For the second pick, we had to look at what robots could potentially be left by the time we would be picking again. 8818 was pretty high on our list but we knew they had missed a few matches, gained some foul points and also had a yellow card. The goal of our 2nd pick was going to mainly be to play defense and shuttle balls to us for shooting. 

# McAllen Day 2

- Day 2 started off very strong as our programmers finally got the PID tuning right for the shooter. Even though we lost our first match of the day, we managed to finish 3-1 to bring our overall record to 9-3 for the event. After playing our last match with 10385, we knew we would work super well together so we locked that pick in right away and seeing how 8818 played some solid defense against 118, we kept our fingers crossed that they would be available for our 2nd pick. After alliance selection, we met with our partners and worked our strategy that we felt would work best for us. In our first few matches, we wanted 8818 to just shuttle balls to us and we and 10385 would just focus primarily on scoring. We also would have a plan where if one of us had a full hopper of fuel either we or 10385 would play pick defense so that the other with the full hopper could score cleanly. That worked great for us in our first playoff match where we won 199-89. In Match 11, we went up against the \#1 seed and we knew it was going to be tough. Unfortunately, our intake pulley had completely melted away the teeth so only the bottom roller was running and our intaking ability was limited and we lost 226-139. But we saw we could keep up with them as the defense from 8818 kept the match close. So we fixed the pulley and then went back out there for Match 13 and won 137-70, punching our ticket to the grand finals. In the grand finals, we didn’t play up to our best due to our intake constantly getting hit and off track. This would pretty much render us useless since we couldn’t intake and score so we swapped to playing defense to try to give ourselves a chance. We lost both finals matches unfortunately but we are happy with our finalists result. We also came away with our 3rd Sustainability trophy in a row. This gives us 58 district points, the most we’ve ever gotten at one event and more than we got combined at our 2 events last year and the year before so we are feeling pretty good about making it to the state championship this year. 

# Breaks

- Throughout the tournament, we had a lot of breaks but none of it was super damaging. The only thing that lowered our performance was our issues with the kicker bar.

1. Broken Absolute Encoder \- our swerve module got some metal shavings in and it cut through the encoder for the turning motor and that was killing our drive in our first 2 matches. We managed to still win those and get 4 RPs in both so it didn’t hurt our record. We had a long break after our send match so we got the module switched out. **Fix: swapped module for a new one, no issues for the rest of the tournament.**  
     
![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_3.jpg)

2. Swerve Turning Motor malfunction \- we aren’t sure exactly what caused this but around match 13, our NEO 550 on our back swerve module (different from the first one) was somehow not turning the wheel fully. If you spun the motor, the wheel would spin super slow but you could also spin the wheel by hand relatively freely and it wouldn’t spin the motor either. We had to drive 1 match with it like this and we still managed to win (Q19). **Fix: swapped the NEO 550 with a new one, no issues for the rest of the tournament.**  

3. Kicker Bar twisting and breaking plate and jamming balls \- the kicker bar started with issues in the qualification matches. We noticed it would get on top of the balls and prevent us from intaking them. In some matches, one side would go down partially while the other side would be on top of a ball, and then it would twist the plate and eventually break it. We broke 2 or 3 plates through the first several matches. We were able to cut new ones quickly but they just kept breaking. The first fix was to try to add some weight to the bar so it would want to go down naturally. In that match, the bar just ripped completely off from one side so we just decided to remove it completely. **Fix: removed kicker bar for the rest of the tournament.**   

![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_1.jpg)

4. Shooter spacers melting into Shooter Motor \- once we got our PID working on the shooter, we noticed our left shooter was not getting the right RPM while the right one was. At the time we didn’t know what was causing it so we split the two (right shooter slightly overshoot, left one slightly undershoot) until we had a big enough break to check it out. Once we did, we noticed that the spacer we had on the shooter motor had melted and pulled into the motor. We didn’t notice it at first and thought someone had just forgotten to put a spacer there so we had added another one. All 3 spacers had melted together once we fully looked at it. **Fix: removed the spacers and replaced them for now, no issues for the rest of the tournament. Future fix is finding a way to keep the spacers from going into the motor.**  

![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_2.jpg)

5. Intake Motor Pulley melted \- during Match 11, our 27T pulley on our intake motor melted on one side. We noticed the pulley was sliding over to that side throughout the event but forgot to check it during lunch. We printed spare pulleys but unfortunately, we didn’t print the intake motor one which is a double-wide one. So we had to cut the old one in half and then use the spare 27T we printed with it. **Fix: cut old pulley in half and use new 27T pulley. No issues for the rest of the tournament**.

![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_5.jpg)

6. Hopper Ramp Cut \- during the playoffs, after we fixed the intake motor pulley, we noticed our hopper ramp was rubbing against our top roller wheels. This might have been a problem before but we didn’t notice it until the playoffs. **Fix: cut a section out of the plate for the wheels to freely spin.**  

![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_6.jpg)
     
7. Linear intake off track \- This had been happening the whole event but it was always a quick fix and nothing we could fully fix without a redesign. It unfortunately cost us our best performance in the finals as it got off track quickly under heavy defense. **Fix: we didn’t fix it as it was the end of the tournament but we are planning to fix it to prevent this from happening.**

![Broken Absolute Encoder](/assets/images/recap_images/62_4.jpg)

We plan to go over our goals at Monday's meeting and see if we achieved them. We think we had a great first event showing and want to improve but we have very little time with our students taking AwtyConnect trips next week so we will need to be smart and efficient in any changes we might want to make before our next event.
